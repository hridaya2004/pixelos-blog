Before applying to add your device to the official PixelOS lineup, make sure you meet the requirements below. These exist to ensure a consistent, high-quality experience for all PixelOS users.

**Failure to meet any of the following requirements will result in immediate disqualification, without exception.**

## Core requirements

1.1. You **must** own the device you intend to maintain. Blind or untested builds are not permitted. Devices with minimal hardware differences from yours may be co-maintained, provided you are also actively maintaining your own device.

1.2. You **should** have a solid understanding of Git and version control practices.

1.3. You **must** have prior experience maintaining this ROM unofficially for a reasonable period before applying. Submitting an application immediately after your first build will result in instant rejection. Sustained unofficial maintenance demonstrates commitment and competence.

1.4. You **must** ensure the ROM is stable enough for daily use before applying. Stability expectations may vary by device — if exceptions apply, explain them clearly in your application.

1.5. You **must** make all device sources used in the build publicly available. This allows the team to verify your work.

1.6. You **must not** include stolen or uncredited commits. Any code taken from other developers must be properly attributed. Failure to do so is both unethical and grounds for disqualification.

1.7. You **must** provide the actual device sources being used. Any attempt to misrepresent your sources will result in instant rejection.

1.8. You **must not** provide false or misleading information on your application. Dishonesty results in instant rejection.

1.9. You **should** have made meaningful contributions to your device sources. This demonstrates active involvement in Android development.

1.10. You **should not** be maintaining an excessive number of ROMs simultaneously. Overcommitting leads to reduced quality across all your maintained projects.

## Qualitative considerations

In addition to the core requirements, the following will be taken into account when reviewing your application:

- Your previous and current development work
